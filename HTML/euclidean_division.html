Euclidean division

Divisions

Euclidean division – or division with remainder – is the process of dividing one integer (the dividend) by another (the divisor), in a way that produces an integer quotient and a natural number remainder strictly smaller than the absolute value of the divisor.

An example is better than a thousand words

Take this pie, we want to divide it into 4 persons, but we have 9 parts. So each one gets 2 parts and there is one left. The quotient is 2 and the rest is 1.

The real representation

The 'pie problem' can be represented with another form : The Euclidean algorithm

First you need to place you element. The number you want to divide on the left and the number dividing on the right

Then you have to ask yourself, how many times is there '4' in '9' ? 4 can be added twice !! So we write 2 under the 4. If the dividend is not included in the number on the left, you just have to also select the digit on the right and

Now you just have to deduce 4 × 2 from 8. In yellow, you have the quotient, and in blue the rest

Finally, you can do it with bigger numbers like 373 by 3
